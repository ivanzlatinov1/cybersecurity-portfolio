This section is _optional_ and can be used to provide a summary of recommendations to the IT manager regarding which controls and/or compliance best practices Botium Toys needs to implement, based on the risk posed if not implemented in a timely manner.

**Recommendations (optional):**

1. **Password Policy Enhancement:**  
   Implement a password policy aligned with current security best practices, requiring a minimum length of at least 8 characters and a combination of upper- and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.
2. **Intrusion Detection System (IDS):**  
   Deploy and maintain an Intrusion Detection System (IDS) within the IT environment to monitor, detect, and alert on suspicious or malicious network and system activities, thereby improving the organization’s security posture.
3. **Encryption of Sensitive Data:**  
   Ensure that Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (SPII) and other confidential customer data are protected using strong encryption both at rest and in transit, in accordance with industry standards.
4. **Access Control – Least Privilege & Separation of Duties:**  
   Enforce the principle of least privilege (PoLP), ensuring that employees are granted only the minimum level of access required to perform their job functions. Implement separation of duties to reduce the risk of fraud or misuse by ensuring critical tasks are divided among multiple roles.
5. **Restricted Access to Sensitive Customer Data:**  
   Access to highly sensitive customer data (e.g., credit card information, authentication credentials) shall be strictly controlled and restricted to authorized personnel only, based on defined roles and business necessity.
6. **Data Backup and Recovery:**  
   Establish and maintain regular backups of critical systems and data. Backups should be securely stored, tested periodically for integrity and restorability, and protected against unauthorized access and ransomware threats.
